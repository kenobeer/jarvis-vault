### 0. Intro / Generational framing
Written two days before turning 21. Pattern: first Covid lockdown started two weeks before 15th birthday. Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine two months before 18th. Iran conflict started 30 days before 21st. The question is not self-pity but how to cope with a generation whose formative political years have been defined entirely by instability — while also acknowledging the enormous privilege of being insulated from the worst of it. "Is all hell breaking loose or did we just catch a few shitty years caused by a Chinese virus, a Russian psychopath, and an American self-tanner?"

### 1. Fermi Paradox
Triggered by an audience question at a Davos event with Demis Hassabis (DeepMind) and Dario Amodei (Anthropic). Fermi asked "Where is everybody?" in 1950 — pointing to the gap between the high statistical likelihood of extraterrestrial life and zero conclusive evidence of it. Main theories:
- The Great Filter: an impossible barrier prevents civilizations from becoming interstellar
- Rare Earth Hypothesis: habitable conditions are a near-unique anomaly
- Dark Forest Theory: civilizations hide because any signal invites preemptive attack
- Zoo Hypothesis: advanced aliens are deliberately not contacting us
- Self-Destruction: civilizations develop the tools of their own annihilation (nuclear, rogue AI) before they can colonize space

Hassabis doesn't find self-destruction logically inevitable. Amodei was mostly fan-boying over Hassabis for 30 minutes with an interesting last minute.

- Documentary: "The Thinking Game" — Google DeepMind / Demis Hassabis
- Discussion: Hassabis x Amodei at Davos (YouTube)

### 2. Mirofish
Open-source AI agent engine that deploys thousands of parallel agents to predict outcomes. Each agent has distinct roles, incentives, memories, and expertise. Example use case: predict oil price movements given a hypothetical attack on a Middle East facility — agents include an oil trader, consumer economist, and IEA policy modeler. They analyze independently, debate, critique each other, converge on a more robust answer than a single LLM output. Known issues: bias and herd behavior. Approach is interesting regardless.

### 3. Portfolio diversification in inflation regimes
When bonds and stocks correlate positively (as they do in inflation-driven downturns), the standard equity/bond hedge breaks. Current alternatives all fail in different ways:
- BTC: too volatile
- Gold: post-rally, currently too volatile to price
- Private credit: won't substitute
- Real estate: locked-up liquidity

When diversification is this hard, market efficiency degrades. Related reads:
- FT Alphaville: "Free lunch is cancelled"
- Young Money by Jack Raines: "Nothing is ever really 'priced in' is it?"
- froots Market Insights: "Tune out the Noise" (German)

### 4. Iran War — Moving Parts
Strait of Hormuz is the obvious pressure point, but the system is more complex: global helium shortage, acute water scarcity in Dubai, decades of geopolitical chess underlying all of it. Article in Minority Report publication ("Why the US Will Lose to Iran") is well-written but takes a very pessimistic and dramatic position — worth reading with distance. Based on Prof. Jiang's game theory framework.
