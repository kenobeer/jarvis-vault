## Threads

### 1. Stefan Krause + Factory Berlin
Stefan Krause: ex-BMW Group CFO, ex-Deutsche Bank CFO, ex-Rocket Internet Supervisory Board, nearly became Apple CFO (4-hour interview with Steve Jobs, who cried mid-interview). Jobs' explanation for Apple's success: worth looking up in the podcast (Business Class).

Now runs Factory Berlin: a company builder supporting not just entrepreneurs but also creatives broadly — "Artists, Designers, Musicians and Makers." Interesting because it mirrors Satya Nadella's thesis (Microsoft CEO) of merging design, product management, and engineering into a single "full-stack builder" role. Also echoes Jim Simons' approach at Renaissance: hire smart generalists, give freedom, create cross-pollination, make everyone partners.

- Podcast: Business Class with Stefan Krause
- Factory Berlin: https://www.factory.inc

### 2. Po-Shen Lo — What You Must Know Before AGI Arrives
Mathematician, former US IMO national coach (2014–2023), Carnegie Mellon professor. Key claims:
- We may already be at AGI — at the point where humans are replaceable in creativity
- Most important skill going forward: ability to synthesize your own ideas
- Surprising second claim: empathy — because working together as humans becomes more important, not less, as AI becomes more powerful

Aligns with the Satya Nadella / full-stack builder thesis above.

- Interview: YouTube, title "What you must know before AGI arrives" with Po-Shen Lo

### 3. Yen Carry Trade
Japan maintained near-zero interest rates following its 1989 market collapse. This enabled the Yen carry trade: borrow Yen cheaply, invest proceeds in higher-yielding assets (mainly US). System cracked in August 2024 when Japan raised rates — S&P 500 dropped 6% in response. Connected concept: equity risk premium and its correlation with Japan's rate policy.

- Video: YouTube (clickbait thumbnail but solid explanation, includes Ray Dalio)

### 4. Nuclear Energy
Long-form article on nuclear as a solution to the coming energy problem. Covers plant architecture through uranium supply chain. Author is pro-nuclear but thorough. Opening line: "Nuclear power killed fewer people in seventy years than coal kills before lunch on a Tuesday."

- Article: TSCS — "There Is No Alternative" (https://substack.com/home/post/p-nuclear)

### 5. Double Prompting / RE2 (Re-Reading)
Study on a prompting technique called RE2: re-reading the prompt before answering improves LLM reasoning performance. Results counterintuitive but mechanistically logical — models benefit from attending to the prompt twice. Worth testing in practice.
