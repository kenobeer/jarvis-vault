## Threads

### 1. Why this blog exists
Two reasons: (1) understand and compress information consumed during the week by forcing it into readable form, (2) counter the tendency to outsource all written output to AI — which improves efficiency but atrophies the ability to put thoughts into words. Writing 100% by hand once a week as deliberate practice. Also: genuine enthusiasm for Substack as a platform and belief it will grow in Europe (currently very US-dominant).

### 2. Jack Raines + Slow Ventures Creator Fund
Discovered Jack via Dan Toomey / Good Work (Morning Brew). Jack writes "Young Money by Jack Raines" — weekly newsletter on thoughts, finance, and how to get the most out of your twenties. Works at Slow Ventures, which runs a Creator Fund: invests in creators with loyal communities in high-value verticals, takes ~10% equity stake in the creator themselves (not a specific project). Holistic approach — interesting model but unclear how equity works in practice.

- Recommended article: https://substack.com/home/post/p-141009695 (Late-20-Something's Professional Dilemma)
- Creator Fund post: https://substack.com/@slowventures (Jack's post on it, unread at time of writing)

### 3. The Death of Spotify
Article by Joel Gouveia in The Artist Economy. Two key insights:
1. Music = tap water: unlike TV (House of Cards = Netflix exclusive), every streaming platform has the same 100M tracks. No real USP, commodity product. One platform halving its price kills the rest.
2. Pool payout problem: all subscription revenue goes into a shared pool, then distributed proportionally by stream count — so a heavy listener's money effectively subsidises the artists their casual co-subscriber listens to.
Solution proposed in article: not convincing.

- Article: https://substack.com/home/post/p-death-of-spotify (The Artist Economy)

### 4. Jan Beckers / Bit Capital
Founder and CEO of Bit Capital, ~€2B AUM across 8 tech-focused funds. Known as an unusually strong stock-picker. Average fund performance ~25-30% p.a., best fund up ~1000% over 8 years. Personally doesn't invest in real estate because 7-8% p.a. isn't enough — would rather not invest at all. Podcast: Business Class (German).

### 5. Morningcrunch — Iran War Macro Scenarios
Three scenarios for Iran war duration and economic impact on Europe, based on a Capital Economics study:
- Base case: minimum +1% inflation, -0.1% GDP growth
- Worst case (months-long conflict with energy infrastructure damage): energy costs 4x higher, inflation forecast ~4.7%
Takeaway: Europe is structurally more exposed than the US in all scenarios.
