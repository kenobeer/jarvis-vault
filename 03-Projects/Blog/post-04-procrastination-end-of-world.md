### 2. Obsidian + Claude MCP
Stumbled across a Twitter post about a system that gives Claude full read/write access to an Obsidian vault via a local MCP server. Motivation: AI memory has always been a black box — no visibility into what the model knows or why. This system makes every session traceable by pushing conversations into notes. ### 3. Hungarian Election
One of the most important European elections in years. Viktor Orbán has been in power for 16 years — started as the liberal hope of post-Iron Curtain Hungary (Fidesz), evolved into what he himself called an "illiberal democracy," closer to a soft dictatorship than anything else in the EU. His opponent Péter Magyar won (or is positioned to win) the election. Podcast recommendation for German speakers: "Der Professor und der Wolf" with Armin Wolf (ORF) and political scientist Peter Filzmaier.

- Podcast on Hungary: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6MBq1paspVU (same link logged above — double-check correct episode URL)

### 4. Jamie Dimon / JPMorgan Shareholder Letter
Annual Dimon letter. He makes his usual case against instability. More interesting: he states three things that would not have been questioned a few years ago but now apparently need to be said explicitly:
1. The US must maintain premier military force in the world.
2. The US must maintain its preeminent economic position and reignite the American Dream.
3. The US must manage foreign economic affairs to strengthen itself and critical allies — so the first two points remain true.

Personal read: Dimon as a proxy for establishment anxiety about US institutional decay. Not a neoliberal fan but respect his risk management instincts and sovereignty.

- Shareholder letter: https://www.jpmorganchase.com/ir/annual-report/2025/ar-ceo-letters#section-3
