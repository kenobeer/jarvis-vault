# Vibe B. — Pitch Script (English, 5 min)

Investors: Markus Aumer (Aumer Advisory, ex-Round2), Benjamin Delic, Matthias Hofreiter (Gateway Ventures Wien), Thomas Plank
Format: 8 slides, ~5 minutes, English
Ask: €35.000 Convertible Note | Cap €800K | 20% Discount | 24 months

---

## Slide 1 — The Moment

> "This is a rooftop terrace in Vienna's first district. 200 square meters, views across the entire city, perfect for an offsite for 15 people. And it's completely unbookable. Not because it doesn't exist — but because no platform has ever given its owner a reason to list it."

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem

> "Executive Assistants spend more than four hours on platforms like Peerspace or Spacebase just to book a single offsite. 200 results. Outdated photos. Hidden pricing. Booking requests that sit unanswered for 48 hours. This isn't a search problem. It's a curation problem — and nobody has solved it for B2B."

---

## Slide 3 — The Solution

> "Vibe B. does it differently. No filter menus. No infinite scroll. You describe what you're planning — 'quiet strategy offsite, 8 people, Vienna' — and you get back exactly three curated matches. Instantly bookable. Standardized contract included. Payment via Stripe."

---

## Slide 4 — Why the Inventory is Different

> "The real differentiator isn't the AI. It's the inventory. Premium asset owners — penthouses, private galleries, architect studios — don't list on Peerspace. The risk is too high, the clientele is wrong. Our strict B2B mandate is the structural key that unlocks this shadow inventory. Other platforms can copy the interface. They cannot copy the supply. That's the moat."

---

## Slide 5 — Market & Business Model

> "Vienna alone: 6,600 MICE events per year, €1.32 billion in economic value. No local incumbent. DACH is the natural next step — Munich, Zurich, Berlin. The model: 12% commission from the host, zero fees for the guest. At an average booking value of €2,000, that's €240 in platform revenue per transaction. For context — Peerspace takes 25 to 30% in total. We're cheaper for everyone, and still profitable from day one."

---

## Slide 6 — Why Us

> "Five WU Vienna students. Between us we've worked in fintech, media startups, and strategic consulting. And we can build the platform ourselves — which means every euro you invest goes into growth, not developer salaries. That's not a minor point. It's the reason this round is €35,000 and not €200,000."

---

## Slide 7 — The Milestones

> "With this funding: months one and two, we incorporate the FlexCo and set up the legal and payment infrastructure. Months three through six, we personally onboard the first 20 premium spaces and go live with the first bookings. By month twelve, we're at €25,000 in GMV and ready for a proper seed round with Speedinvest or aws. These are the metrics that open that door."

---

## Slide 8 — The Ask

> "We're raising €35,000 as a convertible note. Valuation cap €800,000. 20% discount. 24 months. That's enough to prove the model works — and to have a real fundraising conversation twelve months from now. The question isn't whether this market exists. The question is who claims it first. We intend to be that team."

*Pause. Look at the room. Don't fill the silence.*

---

## Closing (after Q&A)

> "Thank you. And I especially mean it when I say — I'm looking forward to the hard questions."

---

## Slide Logic

Each slide answers a question the previous one raised:

- S1: What is this?
- S2: Why does it matter?
- S3: How does it work?
- S4: Why can't it be copied?
- S5: Is it big enough?
- S6: Can this team do it?
- S7: What happens next?
- S8: What do I do now?
